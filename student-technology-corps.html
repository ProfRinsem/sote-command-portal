Multiple pathways, one SOTE.

The Student Technology Corps should not imply that there is only one correct route. Students can enter through a common foundation and then branch into the technical area that matches their course path, interests, and career goals.

Technology Cadet

Entry point for new students and visitors. Learns SOTE language, safety rules, zones, characters, documentation basics, and first mission behavior.

Start First Signal
↓

Help Desk Technician

Builds the service mindset: listen to users, document clearly, troubleshoot symptoms, communicate professionally, and close approved support scenarios.

Branch into a technical pathway

Infrastructure Technician

Networking, cabling, switches, VLANs, racks, Proxmox access, server validation, and Rocket’s Forge operations.

Cybersecurity Technician

Cyber range orientation, logs, defensive thinking, safe investigation, Kingston-guided missions, incident notes, and recovery practice.

Software Development Technician

Command Portal work, scripting, automation, dashboards, APIs, Ask SOTEE features, badge displays, and documentation tools.

Advanced SOTE operations

Mission Control Operator

For students working with SOTE outside class: upper-level missions, operations logs, change documentation, tour leadership, peer support, and approved project ownership.

↓

Certified Professional

Ready to launch: resume-ready skills, portfolio evidence, certification progress, interview stories, professionalism, and internship or employment goals.
