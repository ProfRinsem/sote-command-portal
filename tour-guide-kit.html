Core tour stops

Stop 1

Digital Front Door. “This is the SOTE Command Portal. It stores the story, zones, characters, missions, badges, and eventually student portfolio evidence.”

Stop 2

CEIT 325 Classroom. Point at the student workstations. Explain that the classroom is the access floor where students enter the ecosystem.

Stop 3

Mission Control. Point at Rack 1 and the operations area. Explain observe, connect, diagnose, resolve.

Stop 4

Ace’s Support Command. Explain help desk, ticketing, communication, and workforce readiness.

Stop 5

Rocket’s Forge. Explain the teaching datacenter: Proxmox, servers, storage, templates, Linux, Windows, and infrastructure services.

Stop 6

Cyber Defense Command. Explain safe cyber range practice, defensive operations, and Sir Kingston’s role.

Stop 7

Student Pathways. Explain Technology Cadet, Help Desk foundation, Infrastructure, Cybersecurity, Software Development, Mission Control Operator, and Certified Professional.

Speaker notes by audience

Students

Focus on belonging

“There is more than one route through SOTE. You can support users, build infrastructure, defend systems, or create software tools.”

Donors

Focus on impact

“Students are not just hearing lectures. They are building evidence of real skills before graduation.”

Industry

Focus on readiness

“The output is not just coursework. It is documented troubleshooting, teamwork, technical communication, and proof of work.”

Tour safety notes

Do not discuss passwords, production credentials, sensitive internal IPs, real vulnerabilities, private student information, unapproved screenshots, or anything that would expose CCAC systems. Keep the tour public-safe and story-driven.
